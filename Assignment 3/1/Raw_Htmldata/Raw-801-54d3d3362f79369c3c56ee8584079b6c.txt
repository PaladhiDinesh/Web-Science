<div class="article-body">
    
    
    
    
    
    
  <div id="content-body-wrapper">
    
    <div><a href="http://dailydot.com/tags/donald-trump/">Donald Trump</a> might want to come up with a new way to fund his wall between the United States and Mexico.<p>In a <a href="http://www.cnbc.com/2016/02/08/mexico-wont-pay-single-cent-for-trumps-stupid-wall.html">recent interview with CNBC</a>, former Mexican President Felipe Calderón shot down Trump's oft-repeated idea that Mexico would pay for the construction of a wall along the two countries' border. </p><p>“Mexican people, we are not going to pay any single cent for such a stupid wall,” Calderón said. “And it's going to be completely useless.”</p><p></p><div class="ddgce-embed ddgce-embedded-iframe" contenteditable="false" data-value="eyJ1cmkiOiJodHRwOi8vdmlkZW8uY25iYy5jb20vZ2FsbGVyeS8_dmlkZW89MzAwMDQ5MjMwMSIsInRpdGxlIjoiRmVsaXBlIENhbGRlclx1MDBmM246IE1leGljbyB3b24ndCBwYXkgZm9yIERvbmFsZCBUcnVtcCdzICdzdHVwaWQgd2FsbCciLCJtYXJrdXAiOiI8aWZyYW1lIHNyYz1cImh0dHA6Ly9wbGF5ZXIuY25iYy5jb20vcC9nWldsUEMvY25iY19nbG9iYWw_cGxheWVydHlwZT1zeW5kJmJ5R3VpZD0zMDAwNDkyMzAxJnNpemU9NTMwXzI5OFwiIHdpZHRoPVwiNTMwXCIgaGVpZ2h0PVwiMjk4XCIgdHlwZT1cImFwcGxpY2F0aW9uL3gtc2hvY2t3YXZlLWZsYXNoXCIgYWxsb3dGdWxsU2NyZWVuPVwidHJ1ZVwiIGJnY29sb3I9XCIjMTMxMzEzXCI-PC9pZnJhbWU-IiwidHlwZSI6ImlmcmFtZSJ9"><div class="embed-container" style="padding-bottom: 56%;"><iframe src="http://player.cnbc.com/p/gZWlPC/cnbc_global?playertype=synd&amp;byGuid=3000492301&amp;size=530_298" width="530" height="298" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" allowfullscreen="true" bgcolor="#131313"></iframe></div></div><p>During the Republican presidential debate in October, Trump said that Mexico would pay for his border wall, repeating a claim he has made <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2015/07/31/politics/donald-trump-border-mexico-wall-negotiations/">since late July</a>. Trump has also called undocumented Mexican immigrants “thieves” and “rapists” and claimed that Mexico intentionally sends its criminals to the U.S.</p><p>“We're going to do a wall; we're going to have a big, fat beautiful door on the wall,” Trump said during the debate. “We're going to have people come in, but they're going to come in legally.” </p><p>“Mexico's going to pay for the wall,” he added. “I love the Mexican people; I respect the Mexican leaders, but their leaders are much sharper, much smarter and more cunning than our leader.”</p><p>Trump claimed that “a politician can't get Mexico to pay for a wall, I can.”</p><p>In September, Mexico's current president, Enrique Peña Nieto, <a href="http://www.theatlantic.com/international/archive/2015/09/mexico-donald-trump-immigration/404047/">responded to Trump's rhetoric</a>, saying that his government “fully discredits and condemns any expression of a discriminatory character and [any expression] that specifically hurts Mexicans.”</p>Calderón, who served as Mexico's president from 2006 to 2012, correctly explained that more people are leaving the U.S. for Mexico than the other way around. <p><a href="http://www.pewhispanic.org/2015/11/19/chapter-1-migration-flows-between-the-u-s-and-mexico-have-slowed-and-turned-toward-mexico/#number-of-unauthorized-mexican-immigrants-declines">According to the Pew Research Center</a>, roughly 1 million Mexicans, including U.S.-born children, left the U.S. for Mexico from 2009 to 2014. During the same period, U.S. Census data shows, some 870,000 Mexicans immigrated the U.S.—a difference of 130,000 people.</p><p>These statistics contradict Trump, who has made illegal immigration a focal point of his campaign since his <a href="http://www.dailydot.com/politics/donald-trump-2016-presidential-campaign-announcement/" target="_self">announcement speech in July</a> and claims to have elevated the importance of the issue in the <a href="http://dailydot.com/tags/2016-election/">2016 election</a>. </p><p></p><p></p><p>After further claiming that such a wall, regardless of who pays for it, would be bad for the U.S. economy, Calderón lambasted Trump as “not very well informed.”</p><p>“It is incredible that a quite admirable society like the American society could produce such kind of candidates,” Calderon said. “I cannot understand that. No offense, no offense to America. </p><p>“So Donald Trump … is ambitious but not exactly [a] very well-informed man. I don't want to say ignorant, but he is not very well informed.”</p><p>A Trump spokesperson did not respond to a request for comment on Calderón's statements.</p><p><em>Photo via <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/diversey/23945955973/">Tony Webster</a>/Flickr (CC BY SA 2.0)</em></p><p></p>
