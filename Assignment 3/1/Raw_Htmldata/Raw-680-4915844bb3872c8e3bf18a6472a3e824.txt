 <h2>Presidential candidate <a href='http://edition.cnn.com/2015/12/08/politics/donald-trump-ban-muslims/index.html'>Donald Trump may want to ban Muslims entering the U.S. </a>but he's making millions from business deals in countries where Islam is the main religion.</h2> <p> In his latest personal financial disclosure required by all candidates, and on his web site, Trump lists various interests in the United Arab Emirates, Azerbaijan and Turkey, and two new ventures in Indonesia. </p> <div id="ie_column"> <div id="ie_column"> <div id="quigo220"><!-- ADSPACE: technology/quigo/ctr.220x200 -->

Here's what he declared in predominately Muslim countries: </p> <p> <strong><span class='inStoryHeading'>Dubai, United Arab Emirates</span></strong> </p> <p> The Trump Organization has plans for <a href='http://money.cnn.com/2015/12/08/news/donald-trump-muslim-dubai-golf-club-damac/index.html'>two golf resorts in the Arab emirate of Dubai</a>. In the filing, Trump lists what is described as a "management deal with DAMAC Crescent Properties" and states the underlying asset "value is not readily ascertainable." He lists no income. Neither resort has opened, although one is due to be completed this year. </p> <p> DAMAC said Tuesday it would not comment on Trump's personal or political agenda. </p> <figure class="body_img body_img--780"> <img src="http://i2.cdn.turner.com/money/dam/assets/151208123032-trump-golf-club-dubai-780x439.jpg" width="780" height="439" alt="trump golf club dubai" border="0" /> </figure> <p> </p> <p> <span class='inStoryHeading'>Baku, Azerbaijan</span> </p> <p> In November 2014, Trump <a href='http://www.trump.com/getmedia/764d1ff8-82a9-4040-b9a4-7667ee4e56d6/TrumpInternationalHotelTowerBakuAnnouncementRelease_FINAL/' target='_blank'>announced a partnership to open a luxury hotel in Baku, Azerbaijan </a>on the Caspian Sea. In the filing, Trump lists income of $2.5 million for "management fees" for the Trump International Hotel &amp; Tower Baku. </p> <figure class="body_img body_img--780"> <img src="http://i2.cdn.turner.com/money/dam/assets/151208114650-trump-tower-baku-780x439.jpg" width="780" height="439" alt="trump tower baku" border="0" /> <figcaption class="caption">The Trump International Hotel & Tower in Baku, Azerbaijan, includes 72 luxury residences</figcaption> </figure> <p> </p> <p> <span class='inStoryHeading'>Istanbul, Turkey</span> </p> <p> Trump Towers, Sisli is a 40-story residential tower in Istanbul, Turkey. On his web site, Trump makes it clear <a href='http://www.trump.com/real-estate-portfolio/istanbul/trump-towers/' target='_blank'>he does not own the development,</a> but the owners have licensed his name. In his filing, Trump lists royalty income of $1 million to $5 million. It's standard in the hotel business for brands to license their names for use by local owners. </p> <p> <strong><span class='inStoryHeading'>Indonesia</span></strong> </p> <p> Just a few months ago, <a href='https://www.trumphotelcollection.com/bali/' target='_blank'>Trump announced plans for an ultra-luxury resort in Bali</a> and a golf course in West Java, Indonesia -- his first in Asia.
