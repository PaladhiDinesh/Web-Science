<p>Fresh off being named the league&#8217;s MVP, the Panthers fifth-year quarterback was stymied by an aggressive Denver Broncos defense in his first Super Bowl appearance on Sunday.</p>
<p>It didn&#8217;t get any better off the field, as Newton also walked out during his postgame interview.</p>
<p>&#8220;I don&#8217;t know what you want me to say, I&#8217;m sorry,&#8221; a very dejected Newton said, prior to walking off.</p>

<p>CBS Sports Radio&#8217;s Jim Rome took a shot at Newton following his postgame press conference.</p>
<p>&#8220;Cam&#8217;s presser was weak. If you&#8217;re going to celebrate when you win, you&#8217;ve got to face the music when you lose,&#8221; Rome tweeted.</p>

<p>The 6-foot-5, 245-pound quarterback who scored 45 touchdowns during the regular season &#8212; and five more in the playoffs &#8212; failed to produce a TD running or throwing for the first time this season and the Panthers lost 24-10 in Super Bowl 50.</p>
<p>Newton didn&#8217;t get much help from his offensive line which allowed a Super Bowl record-tying seven sacks, or his wide receivers, who dropped big passes at key times.</p>
<p>Newton spent much of the game under heavy duress as the Panthers were unable to handle the pressure of Von Miller and DeMarcus Ware on the edges. Miller had 2 1/2 sacks, including two strip-sacks against overmatched right tackle Mike Remmers that led to Denver&#8217;s two touchdowns.</p>
<p>Denver&#8217;s first touchdown came when Miller blew past Remmers and ripped the ball out of Newton&#8217;s grasp. Malik Jackson recovered the ball in the end zone for a touchdown to put the Panthers in an early 10-0 hole they could never dig out of.</p>
<p>Newton never looked comfortable in the pocket, side-arming throws, overthrowing wide receivers and generally running for his life.</p>
<p>Eventually frustration began to set it.</p>
<p>With Carolina trailing by six late in the fourth quarter, Miller broke through again and knocked the ball from Newton&#8217;s hands. With the ball rolling on the turf, Newton opted not to dive in the pile and the Broncos recovered at the Panthers 4 to set up C.J. Anderson&#8217;s clinching TD run.</p>
<p>With less than three minutes remaining, Newton was knocked down in the end zone and screamed at an official looking for a late hit but didn&#8217;t get one.</p>
<p>The question surrounding the Panthers all year long was whether their wide receivers would be good enough for Newton after Kelvin Benjamin went down with a season-ending knee injury in training camp.</p>
<p>Carolina still managed to finish No. 1 in the league in scoring this season, putting up 500 points in the regular season and 80 more in the postseason.</p>
<p>But a key drop by Jerricho Cotchery early came two plays before Newton&#8217;s first fumble. Ted Ginn Jr. also had a ball go through his hands and into the awaiting arms of T.J. Ward for an interception that denied Carolina a scoring opportunity.</p>
<p>The Broncos neutralized tight end Greg Olsen and the Panthers lost Philly Brown to a concussion in the second half rendering their offense useless.</p>
<p>The Panthers could never establish a running game with Jonathan Stewart held to 29 yards on 12 carries and one touchdown run while battling through a foot injury.</p>
<p>Newton finished with 265 yards passing and only a 55.4 quarterback rating.</p>
