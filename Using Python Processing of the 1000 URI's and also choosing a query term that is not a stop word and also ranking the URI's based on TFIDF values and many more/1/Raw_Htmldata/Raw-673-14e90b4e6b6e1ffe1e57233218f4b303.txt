                <h1 class="entry-title">Trump on Iowa: I probably came in first</h1>

        <p>By Mariela Hernandez</p>
<p>WASHINGTON (Talk Media News) &#8211; Republican front-runner Donald Trump claimed Monday on CNN that he didn’t lose the Iowa caucus, but rather blamed Sen. Ted Cruz (R-TX) taking votes from Ben Carson.</p>
<p>“I came in second out of the original seven candidates, I don’t consider that a loss,” Trump said. “I probably came in first in actuality.”</p>
<p>The day before the Iowa caucus, the Cruz campaign sent out an email which said that the &#8220;PRESS REPORTING CARSON TO TAKE BREAK AFTER IOWA&#8221; and that the retired neurosurgeon will be &#8220;making a big announcement next week.&#8221;</p>
<p>A claim Carson has denied.</p>
<p>Cruz has since apologized for the “misunderstanding,” but Trump was doubtful if anything will result in Cruz&#8217;s campaign tactic.</p>
<p>Currently, Trump is leading at the top of the polls in New Hampshire by 21 points over Cruz. Trump told CNN&#8217;s Jake Tapper that he hopes to win, but doesn’t think he has to in order to finalize the Republican nomination.</p>
<p>The New Hampshire primaries will begin Tuesday morning for both republican and democrat candidates.</p>
