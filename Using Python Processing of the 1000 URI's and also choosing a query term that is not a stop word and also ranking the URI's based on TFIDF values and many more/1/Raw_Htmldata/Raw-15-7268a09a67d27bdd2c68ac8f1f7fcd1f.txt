							<div class="tab-pane" id="details">
							<h4>Project Details</h4>
								<p><a href="/sites/default/files/projects/2016/factsheet_19thAve.pdf" title="Project Factsheet PDF">Project Factsheet PDF</a></p>
<p>The Project is in its early stages of development. In March 2014, the San Francisco County Transportation Authority (SFCTA) Board adopted the <a href="http://www.sfcta.org/19thave">19th Avenue Transit Study</a> that completed the feasibility study stage of the project by identifying and evaluating feasible alternatives.</p>
<p>The Project has been advancing through the Pre-Environmental Study phase since July 2014, led by SFMTA. This phase will further define the project alternatives and prepare a Project Study Report (a type of “project initiation” document required by the California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) because the project would affect State Highway 1). We anticipate completing this phase in early 2016.</p>
<p><strong>Partner Agencies</strong><br />
Caltrans<br />
SFCTA<br />
San Francisco Planning Department</p>
<p><strong>Funders</strong><br />
Metropolitan Transportation Commission (Priority Development Area Planning Grant)<br />
Parkmerced<br />
SFCTA (Prop K)<br />
San Francisco State University<br />
Stonestown Galleria</p>

<h4><strong>Outreach</strong></h4>
<p>We are hosting a series of public meetings in February 2016 to share and get feedback on the refined project alternatives. There will be a presentation and a Q&amp;A session. An open house will also be available throughout the meeting for more in-depth information.</p>
<ul><li><a href="http://www.sfmta.com/node/189351">Thursday,  February 4, SF State University, 6-8pm</a></li>
<li><a href="http://www.sfmta.com/node/190236">Tuesday, February 16, IT Bookman Community Center, 7-9pm</a></li>
<li><a href="http://www.sfmta.com/calendar/meetings/19th-avenuem-ocean-view-community-meeting-sfmta-headquarters">Thursday, February 18, SFMTA Headquarters, 6-8pm</a></li>
<li><a href="http://www.sfmta.com/node/190256">Thursday, February 25, Waldorf High School, 6-8pm</a></li>
</ul><p>Please contact Liz Brisson, Project Manager (<a href="mailto:liz.brisson@sfmta.com">liz.brisson@sfmta.com</a> or 415.701.4791) or Kelley McCoy, Public Relations Officer (<a href="mailto:kelley.mccoy@sfmta.com">kelley.mccoy@sfmta.com</a> or 415.701.5428) with questions or to request a meeting or presentation to your group.</p>

<h4><strong>Schedule</strong></h4>
<p>The project does not have a schedule for the next phases of work, but major milestones before project approvals would include:</p>
<ul><li>A multi-year environmental review effort</li>
<li>Ongoing community outreach and involvement</li>
<li>Identification of a full funding strategy</li>
</ul><p>After project approvals, the project would be advanced to a more detailed level of design and then move forward to construction.</p>
