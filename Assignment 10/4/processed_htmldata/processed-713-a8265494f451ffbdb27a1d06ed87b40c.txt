Trump Declines to Tell Blitzer How He’ll ‘Knock the Hell Out of ISIS’

   by [21]Lindsey Ellefson | 2:00 pm, February 8th, 2016
   video

   IFRAME:

   ell-out-of-isis%2F&layout=box_count&show-faces=false&action=like&width=
   51&font=arial&colorscheme=light

   Screen Shot 2016-02-08 at 1.52.58 PM Today, during a phoned-in
   interview on CNN, Donald Trump was asked a particularly difficult
   question about ISIS by Wolf Blitzer, but he didn’t expand on it like
   [23]he has in the past. Blitzer shared that there are currently
   presumed to be 60 ISIS fighters on the ground in Europe to carry out
   attacks in cities like Paris, London, and Berlin.

   “If you were President of the United States, what would you do about
   that?” he asked the man with a history of [24]struggling with foreign
   policy.
   Please enable Javascript to watch.

   “I would knock the hell out of ISIS, Wolf,” said Trump. “I would knock
   them out and they would be gone so fast.”

   He went on to decry immigrants and refugees, but when Blitzer asked,
   “Do you want to give us details how you would do that?”

   “I don’t want to do that now, no,” responded the presidential
   candidate. “We aim to have a little bit of surprise every once in a
   while without telling our plans.”

   This interview came two days after the eighth GOP debate, which
   ostensibly served to give each candidate a platform from which to tell
   their plans.
