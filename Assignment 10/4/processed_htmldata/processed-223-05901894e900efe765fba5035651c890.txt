NYC Subway Map Reimagined To Be More Tourist Friendly

   [38]18 comments
   by [39]Zoe Rosenberg [40]@zoe_rosenberg Oct 28, 2015, 1:30p
     * [41]tweet
     * [42]share
     * [43]pin
     * [44]Rec

   [nycsubway4.0.png] Images by Tommi Moilanen via [45]NYO.

   New Yorkers have a lot of love for the New York City subway map, but
   that doesn't mean it's the best version of itselfyet. Tourists and
   locals in unfamiliar locales pore over the map in ire when they find
   themselves on an express train whizzing by their stop with no halt in
   site. Finnish designer Tommi Moilanen has recrafted the subway map in
   an attempt to put an end to that. Moilanen's new version of the mass
   transit map falls somewhere between Massimo Vignelli's largely
   discarded [46]1972 designthe signage his firm created, though,
   [47]still dots every single NYC subway stationand John Tauranac and
   company's 1978 remaking.

   As [48]per the Observer, Moilanen focused on isolating express and
   local lines, demarcating major landmarks like Central Park and the
   Empire State Building, and more clearly identifying station names and
   major transfers. The map also takes the [49](controversial) stand of
   including the PATH train "since it really is a subway type rapid
   transit system connecting New Jersey to Manhattan...[and] also accepts
   Metro Card as does AirTrain JFK which is treated in a similar manner on
   the map," Moilanen explains. Is Moilanen's version a map for the New
   York City of the early 21st century, or is it no better than
   Tauranac's?
