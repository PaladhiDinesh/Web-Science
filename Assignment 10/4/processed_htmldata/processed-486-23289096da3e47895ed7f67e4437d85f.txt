Wal-Mart Heir Gives Clinton $335,000

   Published by [15]tyson at February 4, 2016
   Categories
     * [16]News

   Tags
     * [17]Bernie Sanders
     * [18]Hillary Clinton
     * [19]Minimum Wage
     * [20]Walmart

   Walmart
   Hillary WALLY

   Hillary Clinton on Walmart’s Board of Directors

   NATIONAL (VFB) – Less than two weeks after the world’s largest retailer
   [21]Walmart announced it would raise hourly wages for U.S. workers to
   $10 an hour, election filings released Sunday show Walmart heir Alice
   Walton gave [22]$335,000 to Hillary Clinton’s so-called “Victory Fund.”

   The political donation, given in December, immediately caught the
   attention of Senator Bernie Sanders who wasted no time hammering the
   [23]former Walmart Board Member Clinton.

     A main characters in Sanders' stump speech is the Walton family
     (WalMart). Alice Walton gave Clinton's DNC/state party fund ~$350k
     in Dec.

     — Gabriel Debenedetti (@gdebenedetti) [24]February 2, 2016

   For years, Sanders has been an outspoken critic of Walmart’s massive
   corporate welfare. That’s because the nation’s largest private sector
   employer is also the [25]biggest consumer of [26]taxpayer supported
   aid.

     I got a message for the Walton family of Walmart: Get off of welfare
     and pay your workers a living wage.

     — Bernie Sanders (@SenSanders) [27]January 19, 2016

   As more Americans learn that they’re being forced to
   [28]subsidize corporate greed, the federal minimum wage, which
   currently sits at $7.25/hr, has become a central theme of the election
   (for Democrats).

   Bernie Sanders has [29]consistently advocated for a $15/hr [30]federal
   minimum wage.

     The current federal minimum wage of $7.25 an hour is a starvation
     wage and must be raised to a living wage of $15 an hour.

     — Bernie Sanders (@BernieSanders) [31]January 28, 2016

     .[32]@BernieSanders: "We are going to raise the minimum wage to 15
     bucks an hour." [33]#IowaCaucus [34]pic.twitter.com/2xrrBNIe01

     — FOX Business (@FoxBusiness) [35]February 2, 2016

   On the other hand, Hillary Clinton, who [36]gets paid $300,000 for a
   single speech, is against a $15/hr wage, and has said $12/hr is
   sufficient.

     Sanders calls for $15 an hour minimum wage, Clinton backs $12 an
     hour [37]https://t.co/LMgdqqOQ4z [38]#DemDebate
     [39]pic.twitter.com/D6mSUUIUbg

     — Bloomberg (@business) [40]November 15, 2015

     In spin room, Clinton's campaign manager explains why she supports
     $12 minimum wage, as opposed to $15. [41]#DemDebate
     [42]https://t.co/Ilh1Sk9tWG

     — CBSN (@CBSNLive) [43]November 15, 2015

   Back to the Walmart donations…

   Walmart claims [44]the raise from $9 to $10/hr [45]will cost the
   company $1.5 billion.

   That means:
   A $12/hr minimum (Clinton’s plan) would cost Walmart $3 billion/yr.
   A $15/hr minimum (Bernie’s plan) would cost Walmart $7.5 billion/yr.

   So even though Clinton and the Walton klan [46]go way back, donations
   to her “Victory-fund”  are merely an [47]investment in the retail
   giant’s future. Every year under Clinton’s $12/hr wage plan, Walmart
   would stand to earn an additional $3 billion.

     Walton, Benioff join billionaires backing Clinton in 2016 |
     [48]http://t.co/PGXKjwSemy

     — Bloomberg Politics (@bpolitics) [49]February 1, 2014
