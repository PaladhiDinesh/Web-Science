        <p>After Sen. Marco Rubio got hammered in ABC's New Hampshire GOP Debate, <a href="http://www.politico.com/story/2016/02/marco-rubio-new-hampshire-218923">his poll numbers began to slide:</a></p>
<blockquote><p>An internal poll conducted on Sunday suggests that Marco Rubio’s fumbled debate performance has damaged his prospects heading into the  New Hampshire primary. </p>
<p>The poll, conducted by the pro-John Kasich New Day for America Super  PAC, shows Rubio plummeting to fourth place in the primary here, with 10  percent of the vote. Most of the polling conducted in the immediate  days before the debate showed Rubio in second place. </p>
<p>Donald Trump holds a wide lead in the survey, receiving 35 percent. He  more than doubles runner-up Kasich, who has 15 percent. In third is Jeb  Bush, with 13 percent. Behind Rubio in fifth and sixth place,  respectively, are Christie and Ted Cruz. Both receive 8 percent.</p></blockquote>
<p>In a new <a href="http://www.cbsnews.com/news/poll-trump-leads-four-way-deadlock-for-second-place-in-new-hampshire/">Monmouth poll,</a> Bernie leads by ten points:</p>
<blockquote><p>For likely Democratic primary voters, the Monmouth survey found Vermont Sen. Bernie Sanders held just a 10-point advantage over rival  Hillary Clinton, 52-42 percent -- a slightly closer race than the 53-39  percent results from last month's poll. </p>
<p>Sixty percent of likely  voters said they were completely devoted to their candidate choice.  Twenty-three percent stated a strong preference but still considered  themselves open to a change of heart. Only seven percent had a "slight  preference." Ten percent remain undecided.
</p></blockquote>
<p>There still are a lot of undecided, but Hillary has a hill to climb. Trump still leads big, but Bush has climbed the most.</p>
<blockquote><p>According to a new Monmouth University survey released Sunday, Trump holds a considerable double-digit lead over his opponents, with 30  percent of likely Republican primary voters saying they would vote for  the billionaire businessman.</p>
<p>Four candidates trail Trump in a  virtual tie for second place: Ohio Gov. John Kasich garnered 14 percent  support, Florida Sen. Marco Rubio earned 13 percent, former Florida Gov.  Jeb Bush also drew 13 percent, and Texas Sen. Ted Cruz had 12 percent  support. Most candidates remained unchanged in their numbers from last  month's Monmouth survey, except for Bush, who jumped nine points since  January.</p></blockquote>
