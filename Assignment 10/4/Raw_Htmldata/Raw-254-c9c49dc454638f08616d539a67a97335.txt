<p><img alt="" src="http://i2.wp.com/cdn1.vox-cdn.com/thumbor/9BX8_Wj6SMiKBK__tN1hOL1gfWc=/0x157:3776x2674/1310x873/cdn0.vox-cdn.com/uploads/chorus_image/image/48757507/pic-01.0.0.jpg?w=860&#038;ssl=1" data-recalc-dims="1" /></p>
<p>&#8220;Being miserable and treating other people like dirt is every New Yorker&#8217;s God-given right,&#8221; fictional New York City Mayor Lenny Clotch famously said in 1989&#8217;s <i>Ghostbusters II</i>. So is complaining about every and anything, especially fancy new stuff like cell service in the subway. We&#8217;ve barely had it for more than a year, and already it&#8217;s the source of many complaints. It&#8217;s here for one station, gone the next, which leaves you with barely enough time to post that wicked rejoinder on Facebook.</p>
<p>Along comes <a href="http://www.subspotting-app.nyc/">Subspotting</a>, a new app to help track Wi-Fi service and cell reception for the four major carriers  AT&amp;T, T-Mobile, Verizon, and Sprint  along your daily route. Say you&#8217;re traveling on the Q train from Astoria to Coney Island&#8230;.</p>
