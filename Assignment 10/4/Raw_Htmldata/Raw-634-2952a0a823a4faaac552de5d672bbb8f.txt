<span class="pb-caption">In this July 21, 1992 file photo, then-Democratic presidential nominee Bill Clinton stands with his wife Hillary Clinton during a campaign stop. (AP Photo/Greg Gibson, File)</span> </div> <p>Roger Stone, one-time strategist for Donald Trump, is preparing for a Hillary Clinton nomination in an unusual way, <a href="http://www.reuters.com/article/us-usa-election-clinton-women-idUSMTZSAPEC288843FQ">according to Reuters</a>. Stone plans to form a political action committee which will focus intently on highlighting the story of Kathleen Willey, a former volunteer who accused Bill Clinton of sexual harassment.</p> <p>It's a simple plan with an obvious rationale.</p> <p>In the 2004 campaign, John Kerry had an advantage that George W. Bush couldn't match. A decorated veteran of the Vietnam War, Kerry could draw a sharp contrast with Bush on military service and leadership at a time when the country was focused on foreign policy -- and at a time when U.S. casualties in Iraq were spiking.</p> <p>From which the "<a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/linkset/2007/11/19/LI2007111900952.html">Swift Boat Veterans for Truth</a>" was born. Men who'd served with Kerry stepped forward to suggest that the stories of his service in Vietnam were overblown, putting Kerry on the defensive and sowing doubt that his advantage was actually all that advantageous. Did it cost Kerry the election? No, <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/post-partisan/post/the-swift-boat-myth/2012/07/10/gJQAFyzGbW_blog.html">probably not</a>. But it was a way of turning Kerry's strength into a negative, a tactic warmly embraced by top <a href="http://www.pbs.org/wgbh/pages/frontline/shows/architect/interviews/slater.html">Bush adviser Karl Rove</a>.</p> <div class="inline-content inline-video"> <div class="posttv-video-embed wpv-player" data-aspect-ratio="0.5625" data-category-id="the-fix" data-duration="78000" data-headline="Bill Clinton steps up attacks on Sanders" data-is-truth-teller="0" data-max-height="-1" data-max-width="-1" data-object-id="56b8fcc9e4b0fa2af1a1b764" data-show-endscreen="1" data-show-promo="http://s3.amazonaws.com/posttv-thumbnails-prod/thumbnails/56b8fcc9e4b0fa2af1a1b764/DEM_2016_Bill_Clinton_0c737.jpg" data-uuid="ebc15f12-cea3-11e5-90d3-34c2c42653ac" data-variants="0" data-youtube-id=""><script src="//js.washingtonpost.com/video/resources/env/WapoVideo/dist/WapoVideoEmbed.min.js"></script></div> <div class="inline-video-caption"> <span class="pb-caption">Hillary Clinton is unleashing her biggest weapon on Bernie Sanders: Bill (Peter Stevenson/The Washington Post)</span> </div> </div> <p>This year, Hillary Clinton has an advantage that the Republicans won't be able to match, save a stunning reversal of fortune by Carly Fiorina. If Clinton is the nominee, she will be poised to be the first woman elected to the White House -- a landmark that will almost certainly spur some voters to give her their vote.</p> <p>Enter Stone. His former client, Trump, previewed this line of attack late last year when he started attacking Clinton by noting that Bill had been accused of much more unsavory things.</p> <div class="flex-embed"> <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" data-width="500"> <p dir="ltr" lang="en">If Hillary thinks she can unleash her husband, with his terrible record of women abuse, while playing the women's card on me, she's wrong!</p> <p>— Donald J. Trump (@realDonaldTrump) <a href="https://twitter.com/realDonaldTrump/status/681447548133965824">December 28, 2015</a></p></blockquote> <p><script async="" charset="utf-8" src="//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js"></script></p></div> <div class="flex-embed"> <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" data-width="500"> <p dir="ltr" lang="en">I hope Bill Clinton starts talking about women's issues so that voters can see what a hypocrite he is and how Hillary abused those women!</p> <p>— Donald J. Trump (@realDonaldTrump) <a href="https://twitter.com/realDonaldTrump/status/683378470093746176">January 2, 2016</a></p></blockquote> <p><script async="" charset="utf-8" src="//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js"></script></p></div> <p>The idea, as with the Swift Boats: Get people who were thinking of voting for Clinton because of her gender to reconsider.</p> <p>It's not clear how extensively Willey would be deployed. Per Reuters: "Willey said she will give interviews and speeches and appear in political advertisements to ensure the accusations remain part of the political discourse during the election campaign."</p> <p>Her story, as our Glenn Kessler <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/fact-checker/wp/2015/12/30/a-guide-to-the-allegations-of-bill-clintons-womanizing/">detailed</a> during the Trump-Clinton feud, is a grim one. "The former White House aide said Clinton groped her in his office in 1993," Kessler wrote, "on the same day when her husband, facing embezzlement charges, died in an apparent suicide. ... [A]n independent prosecutor concluded 'there is insufficient evidence to prove to a jury beyond a reasonable doubt that President Clinton’s testimony regarding Kathleen Willey was false.'" (When being deposed in the 1990s, we'll note, Willey denied having been touched.)</p> <p>Stone worked for Trump through the first few months of the businessman's campaign. He left (Trump says he was fired) shortly after the first debate -- and after Trump accused Fox News anchor Megyn Kelly of having "blood coming out of her ... wherever."</p> <p>That Trump reply, of course, came after Kelly asked him about his behavior toward women.</p> <div class="flex-embed"> <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" data-width="500"> <p dir="ltr" lang="en">Sorry <a href="https://twitter.com/realDonaldTrump">@realDonaldTrump</a> didn't fire me- I fired Trump.
