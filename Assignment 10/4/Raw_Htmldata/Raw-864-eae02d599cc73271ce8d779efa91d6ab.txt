		<p>Sure, McDonald&#8217;s food may taste delicious, but it&#8217;s no secret that it&#8217;s not exactly the healthiest. However, one mother in Alaska has performed a McDonald&#8217;s experiment of sorts that is giving us the creeps. . . because it&#8217;s showing us visual evidence of *just* how unhealthy their food really is.</p>
<p>Last week, Jennifer Lovdahl posted a picture on <a href="https://www.facebook.com/photo.php?fbid=10153433396535669&amp;set=pcb.10153433398490669&amp;type=3&amp;theater">Facebook</a> of a Happy Meal. Sounds like an average Facebook post, right? Except this Happy Meal was purchased back in 2010. That&#8217;s right — it&#8217;s been sitting untouched for over six years.</p>
<p>Lovdahl, who works in a chiropractic office, explained that the meal has been sitting in the office the entire time and &#8220;has not rotted, molded, or decomposed at all&#8221;— in fact, &#8220;it smells only of cardboard.&#8221; Yikes.</p>
<p>And, indeed, the food looks pretty much brand new, complete with the January 8th, 2010 receipt. &#8220;We did this experiment to show our patients how unhealthy this &#8216;food&#8217; is,&#8221; she explained in the post. &#8220;Especially for our growing children!! There are so many chemicals in this food! Choose real food! Apples, bananas, carrots, celery. . . those are real fast food.&#8221;</p>
