<p style="text-align: justify;"><strong>NATIONAL (VFB)</strong> &#8211; Less than two weeks after the world&#8217;s largest retailer <span style="color: #0000ff;"><a style="color: #0000ff;" href="http://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2016-01-20/wal-mart-to-give-raise-to-1-1-million-u-s-workers-next-month">Walmart announced</a></span> it would raise hourly wages for U.S. workers to $10 an hour, election filings released Sunday show Walmart heir Alice Walton gave <span style="color: #0000ff;"><a style="color: #0000ff;" href="http://www.cnn.com/2016/02/03/politics/new-hampshire-democratic-town-hall-what-to-watch/">$335,000 to Hillary</a></span> Clinton&#8217;s so-called &#8220;Victory Fund.&#8221;</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">The political donation, given in December, immediately caught the attention of Senator Bernie Sanders who wasted no time hammering the <span style="color: #0000ff;"><a style="color: #0000ff;" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JoCPRSrScG8">former Walmart Board Member</a></span> Clinton.</p>

That&#8217;s because the nation&#8217;s largest private sector employer is also the <span style="color: #0000ff;"><a style="color: #0000ff;" href="http://www.bloombergview.com/articles/2013-11-13/how-mcdonald-s-and-wal-mart-became-welfare-queens">biggest consumer</a></span> of <span style="color: #0000ff;"><a style="color: #0000ff;" href="http://articles.latimes.com/2014/mar/24/business/la-fi-mh-walmarts-dependence-20140324">taxpayer supported aid</a></span>.</p>

<p><script async src="//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js" charset="utf-8"></script></div>

<p style="text-align: justify;">As more Americans learn that they&#8217;re being forced to <span style="color: #0000ff;"><a style="color: #0000ff;" href="http://www.newsmax.com/Newsfront/wal-mart-walmart-welfare-bernie-sanders/2016/02/02/id/712447/">subsidize corporate greed</a></span>, the federal minimum wage, which currently sits at $7.25/hr, has become a central theme of the election (for Democrats).</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Bernie Sanders has <span style="color: #0000ff;"><a style="color: #0000ff;" href="http://www.sanders.senate.gov/newsroom/press-releases/sanders-introduces-bill-for-15-an-hour-minimum-wage">consistently</a></span> advocated for a $15/hr <span style="color: #0000ff;"><a style="color: #0000ff;" href="https://berniesanders.com/issues/a-living-wage/">federal minimum wage</a></span>.</p>
<div class="rve" data-content-width="1200">

<p style="text-align: justify;">Back to the Walmart donations&#8230;</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Walmart claims <span style="color: #0000ff;"><a style="color: #0000ff;" href="http://news.walmart.com/news-archive/2016/01/20/more-than-one-million-walmart-associates-receive-pay-increase-in-2016">the raise</a></span> from $9 to $10/hr <span style="color: #0000ff;"><a style="color: #0000ff;" href="http://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2016-01-20/wal-mart-to-give-raise-to-1-1-million-u-s-workers-next-month">will cost</a></span> the company $1.5 billion.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">That means:<br />
A $12/hr minimum (Clinton&#8217;s plan) would cost Walmart $3 billion/yr.<br />
A $15/hr minimum (Bernie&#8217;s plan) would cost Walmart $7.5 billion/yr.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">So even though Clinton and the Walton klan <span style="color: #0000ff;"><a style="color: #0000ff;" href="http://www.nytimes.com/2007/05/20/us/politics/20walmart.html">go way back</a></span>, donations to her &#8220;Victory-fund&#8221;  are merely an <span style="color: #0000ff;"><a style="color: #0000ff;" href="http://www.boston.com/news/nation/articles/2006/03/12/clinton_feels_heat_over_wal_mart_ties/">investment</a></span> in the retail giant&#8217;s future. Every year under Clinton&#8217;s $12/hr wage plan, Walmart would stand to earn an additional $3 billion.</p>
