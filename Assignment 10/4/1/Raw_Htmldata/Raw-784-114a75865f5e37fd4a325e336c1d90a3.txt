<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
Why these Obama voters are backing Donald Trump
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-978805422644318067' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="el__leafmedia el__leafmedia--sourced-paragraph">
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<span style="font-size: large;"><a href="http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.2365194!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_635/gop-2016-trump.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.2365194!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_635/gop-2016-trump.jpg" height="271" width="400" /></a></span></div>
<div class="zn-body__paragraph">
<span style="font-size: large;"><cite class="el-editorial-source">Farmington, New Hampshire (CNN) </cite>- Eight
 years after they drew inspiration from Barack Obama's insurgent 
campaign, some of the very voters who helped elect the first 
African-American president are standing behind a candidate whose image 
and message are strikingly different: Donald Trump.</span></div>
<div class="zn-body__paragraph">
<br /></div>
</div>
<div class="zn-body__paragraph">
<span style="font-size: large;">Obama
 campaigned on themes of hope and change; Trump declared in his June 
announcement speech that the American Dream was dead. Obama called on 
the country to shed racial divisions; some of Trump's biggest applause 
lines are his pledge to build a wall on the Mexican border and ban 
Muslim immigration. Obama is a gifted orator with a cool and 
intellectual demeanor; Trump is an improviser with a knack for dramatic 
flair.</span></div>
