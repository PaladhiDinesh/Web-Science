<br />
Come see how easy it is to make a snack stadium for your game day celebration! Building plans and free stadium decoration printables are included in this post.<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-rvpQzEfe0ac/VpaR6qPtA0I/AAAAAAAAZ4o/G3wYviErQ2s/s1600/DIY-Game-Day-Snack-Stadium-Plans-Printable.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img alt="DIY Game Day Snack Stadium with plans and free printables #GameDayGlory AD #tutorial #football" border="0" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-rvpQzEfe0ac/VpaR6qPtA0I/AAAAAAAAZ4o/G3wYviErQ2s/s1600/DIY-Game-Day-Snack-Stadium-Plans-Printable.jpg" title="" /></a></div>
It's playoff time and excitement is in the air! We love to have people over to watch games and cheer on our favorite teams. What does every football party need? Great friends and great snacks. This year I decided to celebrate with a fun, DIY snack stadium.<br />
<br />
When I sat down to design the snack stadium, I wasn't sure what size to make it. I realized that size hinged on the materials I used. I considered wood, but that would make for a heavy stadium and require painting. You all know that I am a simple kind of gal, so I wanted my stadium to be easy. I decided to use white foam boards. They are light, easy to cut, and make for easy storage. The standard size of a foam board is 20" x 30", so I knew my size limits. I grabbed some graph paper and started designing.<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-y8kYKWoT42M/VpZ8Doi45JI/AAAAAAAAZ3M/BpDt_4h6UY4/s1600/DIY-Snack-Stadium-Blue-Prints.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="640" src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-y8kYKWoT42M/VpZ8Doi45JI/AAAAAAAAZ3M/BpDt_4h6UY4/s640/DIY-Snack-Stadium-Blue-Prints.jpg" width="508" /></a></div>
I began thinking of how I wanted to decorate the snack stadium. I have access to a printer that makes 11" x 17" prints, so I created large graphics. I decided to cut them in half so that they can be printed on a standard printer. I am offering both the 11" x 17" and 8.5" x 11" graphics as a free download so that you can print and decorate your own stadium.<br />
<h3>
DIY Snack Stadium</h3>
<b>Supplies</b><br />
<b><br /></b> 3 pieces of 20" x 30" foam board<br />
utility tape<br />
large straight edge<br />
pencil<br />
utility knife<br />
stadium decorations (click <a href="https://sites.google.com/a/dianarambles.com/media/home/snack-stadium-decorations" target="_blank">here</a> for download)<br />
clear tape or glue<br />
baker's twine<br />
wooden skewers<br />
toy goal posts and ball<br />
<a href="http://cbi.as/223w0" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">SNICKERS<span style="font-size: x-small;">&#174;</span> Minis 40oz.</a><br />
Skittles<span style="font-size: x-small;">&#174; </span>XL 41 oz.<br />
TOSTITOS<span style="font-size: x-small;">&#174;</span> Medium Chunky Salsa<br />
TOSTITOS<span style="font-size: x-small;">&#174;</span> Original Party Size or Scoops Party Size<br />
Pepsi<span style="font-size: x-small;">&#8482;</span> 20 oz. bottles<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-_7qIi28oles/VpaB7qOBSDI/AAAAAAAAZ3Y/etG_zTbYcBU/s1600/DIY-Snack-Stadium-Supplies.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="518" src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-_7qIi28oles/VpaB7qOBSDI/AAAAAAAAZ3Y/etG_zTbYcBU/s640/DIY-Snack-Stadium-Supplies.jpg" width="640" /></a></div>
<b>Directions</b><br />
<br />
Using your straight edge, mark the pattern on the foam board. You need the following pieces:<br />
<ul>
<li>A (2) 11" x 3" (inside end zone)</li>
<li>B (2) 18" x 3" (inside sideline)</li>
<li>C (2) 11" x 6" (outsize end zone)</li>
<li>D (2) 18" x 6" (outside sideline)</li>
<li>E (8) 1/2 of 3" x 6" (diagonal supports)</li>
<li>F (4) 6" x 6" (outside corners)</li>
<li>optional: X1 (4) 1/2 of 2" x 4" (inside brace for end zone)</li>
<li>optional: X2 (2) 11" x 5" (inside platform for end zone)</li>
</ul>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-psHA2g3XOp8/VpaC4eRobeI/AAAAAAAAZ3g/OkKEmZe9AGY/s1600/DIY-Snack-Stadium-Directions-Mark-Board.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="392" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-psHA2g3XOp8/VpaC4eRobeI/AAAAAAAAZ3g/OkKEmZe9AGY/s640/DIY-Snack-Stadium-Directions-Mark-Board.jpg" width="640" /></a></div>
<div>
Using your utility cutter, carefully cut out the foam stadium pieces along the marked lines.</div>
<div>
Cut out the stadium decorations.</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-r5mzaPNgAWY/VpaD1WF2SVI/AAAAAAAAZ3o/skRlI_tgRLI/s1600/DIY-Snack-Stadium-Assembly.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="404" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-r5mzaPNgAWY/VpaD1WF2SVI/AAAAAAAAZ3o/skRlI_tgRLI/s640/DIY-Snack-Stadium-Assembly.jpg" width="640" /></a></div>
<div>
Assemble your stadium the following way:<br />
<ul>
<li>Attach pieces A &amp; C to form the inside walls of the stadium.</li>
<li>Attach inside walls to the third foam board base.</li>
<li>Attach pieces E to each corner.</li>
<li>Attach pieces B &amp; D to create the outside walls of the stadium.</li>
<li>Attach pieces F to the outside corners to finish off the stadium structure.</li>
<li>Optional: Attach pieces X1 &amp; X2 for inside platform if desired. We had to trim these slightly for them to fit.</li>
</ul>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-JNsQSObutWw/VpaF1NVgEVI/AAAAAAAAZ30/RUSAH44OZgE/s1600/DIY-Snack-Stadium-Tutorial-Steps.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-JNsQSObutWw/VpaF1NVgEVI/AAAAAAAAZ30/RUSAH44OZgE/s1600/DIY-Snack-Stadium-Tutorial-Steps.jpg" /></a></div>
</div>
I used white utility tape along the edges to hold them together. I put it together with small pieces of tape and then went back and used bigger pieces of tape to make the corner edges nicer.<br />
Decorate stadium. I attached the pennants to baker's twine and strung it between skewers stuck into the outside edge.<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-5Hx_3plZgrE/VpaIPZ3GeJI/AAAAAAAAZ4A/YuymEpZDQXw/s1600/DIY-Snack-Stadium-Tutorial-Decorate.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-5Hx_3plZgrE/VpaIPZ3GeJI/AAAAAAAAZ4A/YuymEpZDQXw/s1600/DIY-Snack-Stadium-Tutorial-Decorate.jpg" /></a></div>
Add your snacks and party!<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-Ov6xMRLcUEM/VpaJgRwHfRI/AAAAAAAAZ4M/Hk_xRza0nHo/s1600/DIY-Snack-Stadium-Big-Game-Day.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-Ov6xMRLcUEM/VpaJgRwHfRI/AAAAAAAAZ4M/Hk_xRza0nHo/s1600/DIY-Snack-Stadium-Big-Game-Day.jpg" /></a></div>
Here is how I filled the stadium:<br />
<br />
<ul>
<li>I created&nbsp;Skittles<span style="font-size: x-small;">&#174;</span>&nbsp;Candy Bark* and placed it into the end zone. <i>*recipe below</i></li>
<li>I opened a tall jar of&nbsp;TOSTITOS<span style="font-size: x-small;">&#174;</span>&nbsp;Medium Chunky Salsa and placed it in the middle of the sidelines.&nbsp;TOSTITOS<span style="font-size: x-small;">&#174;</span>&nbsp;Original Tortilla Chips filled the space on each side of the salsa.</li>
<li>I opened jar of queso and placed it to the other side of the stadium flanked by&nbsp;TOSTITOS<span style="font-size: x-small;">&#174;</span>&nbsp;Scoops.</li>
<li>SNICKERS<span style="font-size: x-small;">&#174;</span>&nbsp;Minis were placed in two corners of the end zone and&nbsp;Skittles<span style="font-size: x-small;">&#174;</span>&nbsp;were placed in the other two.</li>
<li>Pepsi<span style="font-size: x-small;">&#8482;</span>&nbsp;20 oz. bottles were placed on the outside corners of the stadium.</li>
<li>Football players were created with&nbsp;SNICKERS<span style="font-size: x-small;">&#174;</span>&nbsp;Minis and soft taffy candies. I unwrapped red and yellow soft taffy candies and microwaved them for about 3 seconds to soften. I cut each in half, rolled each into a little round helmet shape, and place on top of unwrapped the&nbsp;SNICKERS<span style="font-size: x-small;">&#174;</span>&nbsp;Minis. The helmets stuck to the chocolate as it hardened.<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-m1e5KU6mXhA/VpaQHWIbDaI/AAAAAAAAZ4c/MVrqsfq4U2k/s1600/DIY-Snack-Stadium-Snickers-Skittles-Tostitos-Pepsi.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-m1e5KU6mXhA/VpaQHWIbDaI/AAAAAAAAZ4c/MVrqsfq4U2k/s1600/DIY-Snack-Stadium-Snickers-Skittles-Tostitos-Pepsi.jpg" /></a></div>
</li>
</ul>
<div class="hrecipe f1">
<div class="fn single_recipe_header" style="color: black; font-size: 18px; margin: 5px 5px 5px 0px; padding: 0;">
<b>Skittles&#174; Candy Bark</b></div>
<img alt="" class="photo" src="http://www.recipage.com/images/user4168/1452708545/recipe_image.jpg" style="clear: both; float: left; padding: 0 10px 10px 0; width: 40%;" /><br />

<div id="get_media_div" style="clear: both; max-height: 100px;">

<div id="ingredients">
<div class="single_recipe_header" id="ingr_header" style="clear: both; color: black; font-size: 18px; padding: 0; text-decoration: none;">
<b>Ingredients</b></div>
<ul class="single_recipe_text" id="ingr" style="color: black; font-size: 14px;">
<li class="ingredient" style="margin-bottom: 3px;">Skittles&#174; Candy</li>
<li class="ingredient" style="margin-bottom: 3px;">White Candy Melts</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div id="instructions">
<div class="single_recipe_header" id="inst_header" style="color: black; font-size: 18px; padding: 0; text-decoration: none;">
<b>Instructions</b></div>
<div class="single_recipe_header" id="inst_header" style="color: black; font-size: 18px; padding: 0; text-decoration: none;">
<b><br /></b></div>
<div class="instruction">
Melt the package of white candy melts according to the package directions.</div>
<div class="instruction">
Pour the melted candy onto a cookie sheet lined with wax paper or a silicone liner.</div>
<div class="instruction">
Generously sprinkle the Skittles&#174; onto the bark.</div>
<div class="instruction">
Place in refrigerator to harden.</div>
<div class="instruction">
Break into small pieces to create the bark.</div>
</div>
