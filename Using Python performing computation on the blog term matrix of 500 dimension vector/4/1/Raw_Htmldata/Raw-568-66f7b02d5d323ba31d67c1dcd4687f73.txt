              <h1 itemprop="headline">Former Mexican President: We&#x27;re Not Paying for a &#x27;Stupid&#x27; Wall</h1>

            <div class="article_col-main article_col-main-left article_main">
              <div class="article-body" itemprop="articleBody">
                    <p> A former Mexican president had some tough words when asked about GOP candidate Donald Trump's much-touted plan to build a border wall<a href="http://www.nbcnews.com/news/us-news/trumps-border-wall-would-cost-billions-n396551"> </a>that he says will be paid fo<a href="http://www.nbcnews.com/news/latino/donald-trump-announces-presidential-bid-trashing-mexico-mexicans-n376521">r</a> by Mexico. </p>
                
              
              
              
                    <p> &quot;We are not going to pay any single cent for such a stupid wall! And it's going to be completely useless,&quot; said former Mexican president Felipe Calderón when asked about this at the AmCham Egypt for Business Conference on Sunday. </p>
                
              
              
              
                    <p> Calderón had harsh words about the Republican presidential race during his conversation with <a href="http://www.cnbc.com/2016/02/08/mexico-wont-pay-single-cent-for-trumps-stupid-wall.html">CNBC's Hadley Gamble</a>, saying it was &quot;incredible&quot; that quite an &quot;admirable society&quot; like the U.S. had candidates like Trump. </p>
                
              
              
              
                  <figure class="img_half">
                    <noscript><img class="img-responsive img_inline" src="http://media4.s-nbcnews.com/j/newscms/2016_06/1408316/gettyimages-490899414_1aea836a26316306c9ff184647a008b9.nbcnews-fp-360-360.jpg" alt="2015 Concordia Summit - Day 1" title="2015 Concordia Summit - Day 1" itemprop="image"/></noscript>
                    <a href="http://media4.s-nbcnews.com/j/newscms/2016_06/1408316/gettyimages-490899414_1aea836a26316306c9ff184647a008b9.nbcnews-ux-2880-1000.jpg" class="js-lightbox lightbox_link">
                      <button class="btn btn_overlay"><i class="fa fa-expand"></i></button>
                      <img class="js-lazy img-responsive img_inline" src="//sslnodeassets.nbcnews.com/images/transparent-placeholder.gif" data-original="http://media4.s-nbcnews.com/j/newscms/2016_06/1408316/gettyimages-490899414_1aea836a26316306c9ff184647a008b9.nbcnews-ux-320-320.jpg" alt="2015 Concordia Summit - Day 1" title="2015 Concordia Summit - Day 1"/>
                    </a>
                    <div class="js-caption_wrapper">
                      <figcaption class="img-caption img-caption_default no-margin-bottom"><span class="img-caption_txt">Former President of Mexico Felipe Calderon speaks on stage during the 2015 Concordia Summit at Grand Hyatt New York on October 1, 2015 in New York City. </span> <i class="fa fa-camera" aria-hidden="true"></i>Leigh Vogel</figcaption>
                    </div>
                  </figure>
                
              
              
              
                    <p> &quot;No offense, no offense to America. So Donald Trump … is ambitious but not exactly very well-informed man, I don't want to say ignorant, but he is not very well informed,&quot; said Calderón. </p>
                
              
                    <div class="ad-container ad-container_default ad-container_mobile ad-hide">
                      <div class="ad-label ad-hide">
                      	<a href="http://www.nbcnews.com/id/31066137/media-kit/" target="_blank">advertisement</a>
                      </div>  <div class="ad-content ad-xs box1" data-placement="box1"></div>
                    </div>
              
              
                    <p> Trump has made border issues and immigration one of the key centerpieces of his campaign, saying on the day he announced he was running that Mexico was <a href="http://www.nbcnews.com/news/latino/donald-trump-announces-presidential-bid-trashing-mexico-mexicans-n376521">sending &quot;rapists&quot; and &quot;criminals.</a>&quot; The controversial remarks set off a cascade of events including <a href="http://www.nbcnews.com/news/latino/latino-groups-plan-march-saturday-protest-trump-n458676">strong condemnation</a> from the U.S. Latino community - <a href="http://www.nbcnews.com/news/latino/gop-hispanics-warn-against-demonizing-latinos-debate-n450406">including Latino Republicans</a> - and the severing of several business relationships i<a href="http://www.nbcnews.com/politics/2016-election/nbc-cuts-business-ties-donald-trump-over-immigration-remarks-n383856">ncluding one with NBC</a>. Despite the controversy, Trump has escalated his rhetoric on immigration issues and has called for a ban on Muslims entering the U.S. to combat terrorism. </p>
                
              
              
              
                    <p> The former Mexican president said &quot;the first loser of such a policy would be the United States - If this guy pretends that closing the borders to anywhere either for trade [or] for people is going to provide prosperity to the United States, he is completely crazy.&quot; </p>
                
              
              
              
                    <p> Calderón also took issue with Trump's statements that illegal immigration from Mexico is a big and escalating problem. According to a Pew Research report in November, more Mexicans were <a href="http://www.pewhispanic.org/2015/11/19/more-mexicans-leaving-than-coming-to-the-u-s/">emigrating</a> from the U.S. than entering. </p>
                
              
              
              
                    <p> <strong>Relted: <a href="http://www.nbcnews.com/news/latino/more-mexicans-leaving-coming-u-s-new-report-n466226">More Mexicans Leaving Than Coming to U.S. - New Report</a></strong> </p>
                
              
              
              
                    <p> &quot;They don't want to go, they can work for a motor company (that's) not in Detroit, I am sorry to say,&quot; said Calderón about the fact that fewer Mexicans want to go to the U.S. &quot;They are working for a motor company in Hermosillo and Toluca, so Mazda is coming to Mexico, Honda is coming to Mexico. Those kids have jobs in that industry in Mexico.&quot; </p>

                      <div class="ad-label ad-hide">
