Trump on Iowa: I probably came in first

   By [30]Washington Desk -
   February 8, 2016
   SHARE
   Facebook
   Twitter
   Presidential Candidate Donald Trump (R), orders his security to escort
   rowdy protesters out of the University of Iowa Field House. Iowa City,
   Jan. 26, 2016. (Photo: Danielle Wilde)

   By Mariela Hernandez

   WASHINGTON (Talk Media News) – Republican front-runner Donald Trump
   claimed Monday on CNN that he didn’t lose the Iowa caucus, but rather
   blamed Sen. Ted Cruz (R-TX) taking votes from Ben Carson.

   “I came in second out of the original seven candidates, I don’t
   consider that a loss,” Trump said. “I probably came in first in
   actuality.”

   The day before the Iowa caucus, the Cruz campaign sent out an email
   which said that the “PRESS REPORTING CARSON TO TAKE BREAK AFTER IOWA”
   and that the retired neurosurgeon will be “making a big announcement
   next week.”

   A claim Carson has denied.

   Cruz has since apologized for the “misunderstanding,” but Trump was
   doubtful if anything will result in Cruz’s campaign tactic.

   Currently, Trump is leading at the top of the polls in New Hampshire by
   21 points over Cruz. Trump told CNN’s Jake Tapper that he hopes to win,
   but doesn’t think he has to in order to finalize the Republican
   nomination.

   The New Hampshire primaries will begin Tuesday morning for both
   republican and democrat candidates.
