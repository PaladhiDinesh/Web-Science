Wal-Mart to add 10 wholesale stores in Karnataka: Krish Iyer

   Wal-Mart India could spend Rs500 crore in opening large-format
   wholesale stores in Karnataka
   ____________________ [BUTTON Input] (not implemented)__Subscribe to our
   newsletter.
     __________________________________________________________________

   [69]Suneera Tandon
   Photo: Bloomberg
   Photo: Bloomberg

   Bengaluru: Wal-Mart Stores Inc. will add 10 wholesale stores in
   Karnataka.

   Speaking on the sidelines of Invest Karnataka 2016 in Bengaluru, Krish
   Iyer, chief executive and president at Wal-Mart India Pvt. Ltd, said
   that stores in cities such as Bengaluru, Mysuru and Hubli could open
   three years from now.

   “We are yet to buy land, but we’ve signed an MoU (memorandum of
   understanding) with the government and we will expand in this market,”
   added Iyer.

   Wal-Mart India could spend as much as Rs.500-Rs.600 crore in opening
   large-format wholesale stores in the state with investments per store
   varying from $8 to $10 million.

   “We can see it (the stores) doubling over the next decade,” he added.
