Subway takes a stand against battery cages!

   Subway takes a stand against battery cages!
   [28]IMAGE [tri.png]

We are witnessing history in the making. Not even one week after
[29]McDonald’s announced its landmark commitment to dump cage eggs, Subway
Australia is following suit.

   [30][fbshare_hack.jpg] Facebook Share
   [31][tweet_hack.jpg] Twitter Share
   shadow

   LAST UPDATED: 24 February 2016

   Last week there was no end in sight for caged hens whose ‘job’ it is to
   lay eggs for Australia’s fast food giants. Now, two epic phase-outs
   have been announced, which in time will free millions of hens from a
   life of misery in battery cages!

   Fast food chains have been under increased pressure to abandon cage
   eggs after McDonald’s made the big shift in response to [32]a concerted
   social media campaign. Caring Facebook citizens wasted no time by
   ensuring Subway knew that they needed to do likewise:
   Comments flood Subway's facebook page.

   Subway [33]announced the good news on their Facebook page today. This
   decision helps to bring Subway Australia in line with Subway in Europe,
   the U.S. and Canada, where cage eggs are already gone or being phased
   out.

   With a whopping 1,400 stores nation-wide, Subway Australia’s decision
   to remove all cage eggs from its stores over the next 12-18 months is
   another nail in the coffin of Australia’s brutally cruel cage egg
   industry.

   Unlike many other countries, Australian laws haven’t acknowledged the
   cruelty of  [34]battery cages. Fortunately, most Australians have — and
   big business is starting to get the message, too. Subway’s decision —
   combined with similar commitments by [35]McDonald’s, [36]Coles,
   Woolworths, and [37]Nestle — will re-shape the Australian egg industry
   and weaken support for a cruel ‘farming’ system that should never have
   existed in the first place.

   Thank you, Subway!

   With our laws failing to protect animals, corporations are increasingly
   recognising the power they have to end the worst abuses faced by
   animals [38]in factory farms. Thank you, Subway, for this positive step
   in the right direction!

   Thank YOU!

   This latest milestone shows that when caring individuals speak up, even
   corporate giants can be moved to listen. So thank you. No email,
   letter, phone call or facebook message you post on behalf of animals is
   ever wasted!

Help speed the day when all animals are free from factory farms:

    1. Phase out cruelty from your kitchen: [39]click here to download our
       egg labelling cheat sheet and find out what ‘cage’, ‘cage free’ and
       ‘free range’ really means for hens.
    2. [40]Chip in a few dollars to help us expose factory farming to the
       masses – our public awareness initiatives are currently reaching
       one million Australians daily. And it’s having a big impact!
       [41]With your support, we can reach more hearts and minds, and free
       animals from factory farms, sooner.
