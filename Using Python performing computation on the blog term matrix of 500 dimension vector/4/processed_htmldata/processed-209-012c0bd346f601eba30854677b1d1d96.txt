Subway, Pizza Hut, Dunkin Donuts Re-evaluate Pricing

   [6][BK.png]
   Success does leave clues and when customer counts decline, sales become
   stagnant, and competitors are garnering market share it’s time to
   reevaluate just what you are doing according to [7]Foodservice
   Solutions®, Grocerant Guru®. Regular readers of this blog know that
   [8]companies place limits on success, customers do not.   Our own
   Grocerant Guru® went on to say more often than not competition drives
   change.
   Acclaimed Harvard Professor Michael Porter when speaking on
   [9]competition stated that “Industry boundaries are expanding, and they
   can expand even more.  We’re going to see a combination of barriers to
   entry going up and the opportunity for disruption going up
   simultaneously.”  If restaurants are careful that may end up looking
   like [10]Burger Chef or Kodak.
   In 2016 Price has become a barrier in retail foodservice according to
   Foodservice Solutions® team.  Consumers have established new a price,
   value, service equilibrium which our team unveiled in late 2015 after
   following the [11]undercurrents of consumer consumption patterns
   evolving.
   Here is Foodservice Solutions® New Price, Value, Service Equilibrium
   Formula:
   (Mobile Access + Digital Payment +Delivery) X (Price + Food Quality +
   Speed) = Value
    Incremental Value:  Constantly Changing Menu (Seasonally /
   Sustainability with Creditability).
   Subway’s customers are pushing back on social media after they
   announced that they were now selling the selling the Subway
   [12]footlong submarine for $6.00.  They were not happy about [13]the
   loss of Subway’s $5 Footlong, and they’re [14]speaking out via social
   media posts.    Does your company understand how to integrate a [15]two
   tiered pricing plan? Are you utilizing the Price, Value, Service
   Equilibrium?
   Competitive forces have driven Dunkin Brands to [16]miss top line sales
   goals and bottom line profits.  Now Dunkin is [17]revaluating pricing
   and product mix.  Yum Brands, Pizza Hut unit gained traction with $5
   pizza mix and match; yes grocerant niche mix and match works.  After
   they [18]repositioned product pricing to drive customer counts, sales
   and profits they regained sales momentum.

   operator, consultant, brand / product positioning expert, and public
   speaking he will provide success clues for all.
