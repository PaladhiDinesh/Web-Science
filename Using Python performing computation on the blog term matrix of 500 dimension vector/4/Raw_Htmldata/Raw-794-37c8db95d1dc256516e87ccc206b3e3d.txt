When their Philadelphia home was condemned, they moved out of the city, a decision Birnbaum said sparked symptoms of depression for his mother. As an undergraduate, he suffered a breakdown that led to a failed suicide attempt.</div><div class="zn-body__paragraph">Seeking greener pastures, the family moved to the home near the beach in Atlantic City.</div><div class="zn-body__paragraph">"This represented heaven to them. It lifted their spirits," Birnbaum said, looking toward the ocean from the home's living room. "This represented ownership in the greatest country in the world."</div><div class="zn-body__paragraph">Birnbaum used the home as a place to take care of his aging parents.</div><div class="zn-body__paragraph">Charlie's father died in 1988. "We were able to create some quality of life. And that's the memory that far outweighs what happened here in that terrible moment."</div><div class="zn-body__paragraph"><a href="http://www.cnn.com/2015/05/13/politics/being-moody-south-carolina-2016/index.html">Being Moody: This question stumps Republicans</a></div><div class="zn-body__paragraph">Since that time, Birnbaum, who lives with his wife in nearby Hammonton, has relied on the property as the base of his piano-tuning business to house his tools, and for rest between shifts. (CRDA's attorney has pointed to the fact that he doesn't live in the home as partial justification for the eminent domain.) But Birnbaum said that it doesn't matter whether he lives in it or not.</div><div class="zn-body__paragraph">"People all their life are looking for a special place to be. Well, I have it in this place," he said. "I may lose it, it may be gone next year, but I'm going to be able to sleep at night saying I didn't just let them bulldoze it without a fight. And that's something that's going to be with me until the day I'm gone."   </div><div class="zn-body__paragraph">Despite Revel's failure, the CRDA still moved ahead with plans to seize land for the project.</div><div class="zn-body__paragraph">Until then, and until the legal process sorts out, Birnbaum still has access to the house, where he will remain playing his Yamaha baby grand piano.</div><div class="zn-body__paragraph">"I want to see it come up," Birnbaum said of the city's revitalization.
