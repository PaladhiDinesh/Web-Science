<p class="MsoNormal">Those shopping in our stores in China see lots of Chinese New Year decorations and traditional foods stocked for this busy time. For readers who arent in China, heres some background on the celebrations. <o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><b>Traditional Family Meals<o:p></o:p></b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">Before the first day of the first month in the lunar calendar, people all over China travel to their hometowns to unite with their families and decorate their homes in red  a color that symbolizes good luck and joy  and prepare for Chinese New Year celebrations. The night before the Chinese New Year, we prepare a <a href="http://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/collection/chinese-new-year" title="Link: http://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/collection/chinese-new-year" target="_blank" data-cms-ai="0">feast</a> made up of symbolic foods:<o:p></o:p></p>

<ul><li>In Chinese culture, a fish course represents wealth in the future, while peanuts signify longevity and good health.</li><li>Some food symbolism in Chinese New Year dishes is more visual, such as hot pot, which involves simmering meat and vegetables in a round pot at the center of the table. The shape of the pot represents perfection and satisfaction.</li><li><a href="http://travel.cnn.com/shanghai/life/ultimate-guide-chinese-dumplings-466494/" title="Link: http://travel.cnn.com/shanghai/life/ultimate-guide-chinese-dumplings-466494/" target="_blank" data-cms-ai="0">Dumplings</a> are an example of a food with a more historical tie because they resemble the gold currency  Yuanbao  used in ancient China. Today, dumplings are still thought to signify wealth in the coming year and are a delicious treat stuffed with different fillings.</li></ul><aside class="text-center">
    <style type="text/css">#i6d1afb15bed2443991fb68eff5304ac4{display:inline-block;overflow:hidden;position:relative;width:603px;height:nullpx;}</style><span id="i6d1afb15bed2443991fb68eff5304ac4"><img src="http://cdn.corporate.walmart.com/dims4/WMT/7c06c44/2147483647/crop/3008x2008%2B0%2B0/resize/603x603%3E/quality/90/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fcdn.corporate.walmart.com%2F2b%2Fdf%2Fecb557d0431b9960cf0ffb8f3ea2%2Fcenter-aisle-of-walmart-china-store.JPG" width="603" height="403" alt="The center aisle of a Walmart China store" data-size="blog-post-image"></span></aside>
<p class="MsoNormal"><b>Celebrations<o:p></o:p></b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">Like with New Years Eve in the U.S. and other western countries, Chinese New Year involves staying up late. We light firecrackers at midnight, a tradition that dates back to ancient folklore. Though the New Year is a cause for celebration now, legend has it that Chinese villagers used to stoke their fires with bamboo to keep away a terrifying, sharp-toothed monster that arose from the sea at the end of the lunar year to prey on people and livestock. Now, we use firecrackers to celebrate the new year and also scare off any bad luck that might be on the horizon. <o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">Celebrations culminate in the <a href="http://www.travelchinaguide.com/essential/holidays/lantern.htm" title="Link: http://www.travelchinaguide.com/essential/holidays/lantern.htm" target="_blank" data-cms-ai="0">Lantern Festival</a>, where people gather to admire the illuminated lanterns (some floating, some carried by children, some fixed as decorations) and guess riddles written on them. On New Years Day, people also watch lion dances, in which participants don elaborate, mythical lion costumes that seem larger than life  and eat rice dumplings. <o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">One of our family traditions is for children and grandchildren to wish elders in the family good wishes for the new year and, in turn, the elders will give children a red envelope of money for good luck and to buy toys and books.&nbsp; Children often sleep with the red envelope under the pillow to bring good luck throughout the year. <o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><b>The Year of the Monkey<o:p></o:p></b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">This year is the <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2016/01/31/world/gallery/year-of-the-monkey-lunar-new-year/index.html" title="Link: http://www.cnn.com/2016/01/31/world/gallery/year-of-the-monkey-lunar-new-year/index.html" target="_blank" data-cms-ai="0">year of the monkey</a>, the ninth of 12 animals in the recurring 12-year Chinese zodiac cycle. People born in the year of the monkey are believed to be energetic, witty and mischievous. I look forward to greeting the year of the monkey surrounded by my family and enjoying the snacks and festivities that come with the celebrations. No matter your Chinese zodiac, may the New Year bring good fortune to you and your family!</p></div>

    <div class="article-header">
        <div class="article-header-inner">
            <a href="http://blog.walmart.com/topics/innovation" class="article-topic-title category" data-cms-ai="1">Innovation</a><h1 class="article-title">How Lab 415-C is Changing the Shopping Experience</h1>

            <div class="article-byline">
                <div class="article-byline-text">
                    <p class="article-byline-author">By
                        <a href="http://corporate.walmart.com/search?q=Tom Douglass" data-cms-ai="1">Tom Douglass</a>
                    </p>

                    <p class="article-byline-text">Director &ndash; Lab 415-C, Walmart Technology</p>
                    <p class="article-byline-date date">April 28, 2016</p>
                </div>
            </div>
        </div>

        <div class="article-lead-media">    
    <img src="http://cdn.corporate.walmart.com/dims4/WMT/05ac418/2147483647/crop/1500x651%2B0%2B121/resize/1300x564%3E/quality/90/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fcdn.corporate.walmart.com%2F72%2Fa1%2F681addee4429b80df3875a812c55%2Fresizes%2F1500%2Flab-415-c-exterior.png" class="article-lead-img" data-size="blogPost_1300x564"></div>
</div>

    <div class="article-body">
        <div class="article-content">
            <p class="article-intro">
                    Technology is changing the way people live. </p>
            <p class="MsoNormal"><span>At Walmarts Lab 415-C, we look for disruptive,
innovative technology that has the potential to change the way people shop. But
not just change that part of their lives  make it better. <o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">From augmented reality to robotics, our team discovers
and tests emerging technology that powers the shopping experience our customers
want. In fact, were even named after an early innovation in Walmarts
heritage: <a href="http://www.walmartmuseum.com/explore/#/search/query/415/artifact/27917294243" title="Link: http://www.walmartmuseum.com/explore/#/search/query/415/artifact/27917294243" target="_blank" data-cms-ai="1">Sam
Waltons 415-C airplane</a><span>, which he used to scout real estate
from the sky (a business-growth tactic that was unheard of in the 1950s).<o:p></o:p></span></p><aside class="text-center">
    <style type="text/css">#i2ecd34b80526408eac298187168a100c{display:inline-block;overflow:hidden;position:relative;width:603px;height:nullpx;}</style><span id="i2ecd34b80526408eac298187168a100c"><img src="http://cdn.corporate.walmart.com/dims4/WMT/d4d03c1/2147483647/crop/2000x1333%2B0%2B0/resize/603x603%3E/quality/90/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fcdn.corporate.walmart.com%2F3f%2F02%2Fa29944494c9f8b79f04346601557%2Fan-associate-scans-vegetables-for-grocery-pickup.jpg" width="603" height="402" alt="An associate scans vegetables for grocery pickup" data-size="blog-post-image"></span></aside>
<p class="MsoNormal">How do we bring these innovations to our customers and
associates? It starts with research. You wouldnt buy a car without researching
its capabilities, safety and reliability, right? We research between 700-750
technologies a year and make sure we know the technologys maturity, use cases,
comparisons and how it can improve shopping for customers. We look at
everything from technology that helps associates run stores more efficiently to
capabilities in <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internet_of_Things" title="Link: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internet_of_Things" target="_blank" data-cms-ai="1">the internet of
things</a><span> (connected devices that communicate without human
interaction, such as a smart thermostat).<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">But what good is research if it isnt shared with others?
Thats where our showcasing team comes in. More than 5,000 people come through Lab
415-C each year. Our showcasing team helps plan discussions, brainstorms and
events within Walmart and the greater entrepreneurial and academic community to
accelerate how we find innovative technology.</p><aside class="text-center">
    <style type="text/css">#id0adca1aff8f4c40a913579818b5d7ca{display:inline-block;overflow:hidden;position:relative;width:603px;height:nullpx;}</style><span id="id0adca1aff8f4c40a913579818b5d7ca"><img src="http://cdn.corporate.walmart.com/dims4/WMT/7e2e637/2147483647/crop/1500x1000%2B0%2B0/resize/603x603%3E/quality/90/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fcdn.corporate.walmart.com%2F44%2F84%2F33b8ae5f4694ad4f3c3c8a47557f%2Fwalmart-supercenter.jpg" width="603" height="402" alt="Walmart Supercenter" data-size="blog-post-image"></span></aside>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span>Testing technologies for how theyll work within Walmart
is another aspect of Lab 415-Cs capabilities. Weve tested technologies internationally,
at local stores and within our lab at Walmarts headquarters in Bentonville,
Arkansas. Failing fast is key, so we dont see failure as a roadblock; we see
it as a way to finesse the solution to fit Walmarts needs.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">An important way we find solutions that fit our
customers needs is by sourcing innovations from technology suppliers. This October,
we are doing that in a big way through our <a href="http://www.nwatechsummit.com/#!walmart-technology-open-call/raaph" title="Link: http://www.nwatechsummit.com/#!walmart-technology-open-call/raaph" target="_blank" data-cms-ai="1">Technology
Innovation Open Call</a><span>, an event where our leaders will meet with companies
creating the latest technology for retail, logistics, big data, security and
social media. &nbsp;<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><span>Our open call event is a great opportunity for companies
to pitch their innovations to the largest retailer in the world. I cant wait
to see what ideas and inventions well discover! Together we will transform the
shopping experience.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><i>Submission
deadline for potential selection in the Technology Innovation Open Call is July
22, 2016, or the first 250 submissions. For details and an application, </i><a href="http://www.nwatechsummit.com/#!walmart-technology-open-call/raaph" target="_blank" data-cms-ai="1"><i>click here</i></a><i><span>.<o:p></o:p></span></i></p></div>

            <div class="article-byline">
                <div class="article-byline-text">
                    <p class="article-byline-author">By

                <div class="video-container">

    <div class="article-body">
        <div class="article-content">
            <p class="MsoNormal"><i>Editors Note: With
this post, we follow up with two associates who previously shared their stories on video.<o:p></o:p></i></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">Proximity brought Nicholas Qualman to Walmart, but his
personal drive has since taken him far.<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">In 1998, the then 16-year-old was working for a fast-food
chain located in the parking lot of the Walmart store in his hometown of
Marinette, Wisconsin. He was tired of making burgers and wanted to work the
counter, but with no positions open, he had to look elsewhere for a new
challenge. <o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">He applied at Walmart and was hired as a cashier, and he
hasnt stopped moving since.&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">By the time he was featured <a class="" href="http://news.walmart.com/media-library/videos/youtube/nicholas-lpolvdnc7fa" title="Link: http://news.walmart.com/media-library/videos/youtube/nicholas-lpolvdnc7fa" data-cms-ai="3">in this 2011 video</a>, hed earned
10 promotions. After that, he lost count.</p><p class="MsoNormal">Ive had many careers within the same company, he said, reciting
every title he has held, which comes to about 16.<o:p></o:p></p><p class="MsoNormal">His ambition has taken him from cashier to department
manager to a role leading education for other associates and many  many  points
in between. In the summer of 2015, he began helping to support the rollout and
day-to-day operations of <a href="http://grocery.walmart.com/usd-estore/m/home/anonymouslanding.jsp" title="Link: http://grocery.walmart.com/usd-estore/m/home/anonymouslanding.jsp" target="_blank" data-cms-ai="3">online
grocery</a>, which includes <a href="http://blog.walmart.com/innovation/20160413/sharing-the-love-for-grocery-pickup" title="Link: http://blog.walmart.com/innovation/20160413/sharing-the-love-for-grocery-pickup" data-cms-ai="3">store
pickup</a> and <a href="http://blog.walmart.com/innovation/20151208/in-the-news-comparing-online-grocery-services" title="Link: http://blog.walmart.com/innovation/20151208/in-the-news-comparing-online-grocery-services" data-cms-ai="3">home
delivery</a>  a job that he says is his favorite thus far.</p><aside class="text-center">
    <style type="text/css">#ia1723b48fe1046e9853bdbf3385ba1f5{display:inline-block;overflow:hidden;position:relative;width:603px;height:nullpx;}</style><span id="ia1723b48fe1046e9853bdbf3385ba1f5"><img src="http://cdn.corporate.walmart.com/dims4/WMT/bdab0f4/2147483647/crop/5760x3840%2B0%2B0/resize/603x603%3E/quality/90/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fcdn.corporate.walmart.com%2F57%2F68%2Ff4fcd00c436991955bbce9e6aac4%2Fa-customer-checks-in-at-the-grocery-pickup-kiosk.jpg" width="603" height="402" alt="A customer checks in at the grocery pickup kiosk" data-size="blog-post-image"></span></aside>
<p class="MsoNormal">Its a completely new way of us serving the customer, he
said. I equate it with being the supercenter of this generation. Its a game
changer for stores and for our customers.<o:p></o:p></p><p class="MsoNormal">As Nick moved up in the company, he also moved around. One
of the great things is you get to experience different people and the company
in different geographies, he said.<o:p></o:p></p><p class="MsoNormal">He transferred from Northeast Wisconsin to Minneapolis for
college, then worked in Sacramento; San Diego; Los Angeles; Princeton, New
Jersey; Boston; and Scottsdale, Arizona. He now calls San Bruno, California,
home.<o:p></o:p></p><p class="MsoNormal"><b>Like Brother, Like
Sister</b> <o:p></o:p></p><p class="MsoNormal">Nicks drive can only be matched by that of his sister,
Jessica Crow. It took her only five years to do what he did in 17 years, Nick
said with pride and a bit of brotherly frustration.<o:p></o:p></p><p class="MsoNormal">Were kind of in competition, he said, and Ive got to
tell you, Im struggling to keep ahead. Shes told me several times she wants
my job.<o:p></o:p></p><p class="MsoNormal">Jessica joined the military after college and served in Iraq
and Afghanistan. When she returned to the States, she toured the country with
the Pentagon to share her experiences.</p><aside class="pullquote">
    <p>We&#39;re kind of in competition, and I&#39;ve got to tell you, I&#39;m struggling to keep ahead.</p>
    </aside>



<p class="MsoNormal">Despite what shed gained in the military, finding a
satisfying job in the private sector was difficult, Nick said. She worked in
logistics but didnt feel happy or challenged. Thats when Nick offered to share
her resume within Walmart. But, he told her he wouldnt push it: Getting hired
was up to her.</p><p class="MsoNormal">It wasnt long before Jessica was offered the position of
developmental store manager. She made it to store manager in three months and moved
to a new store after a year and half. A few promotions later, she is now a
divisional manager  also surpassing the story she shared in this 2013 video.</p><div class="videoContainer enhancement ">
	<iframe src="//www.youtube.com/embed/NVD5f2neF6I?rel=0&wmode=opaque&showinfo=0&autohide=1" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe>
</div><p class="MsoNormal">After talking about his sister, Nick was quick to point out,
My story isnt unique  its one of many, many stories of Walmart associates.
Not everyone has had a chance to tell their story.<o:p></o:p></p><p class="MsoNormal">Nick doesnt want his story to end here. He achieved his
last goal of joining the e-commerce team, and now hes setting his sights on <a href="http://corporate.walmart.com/our-story/our-business" title="Link: http://corporate.walmart.com/our-story/our-business" data-cms-ai="3">Walmart
International</a>, the one area he says he hasnt yet touched. For now, Nick
sees himself sticking with <a href="http://grocery.walmart.com/usd-estore/m/home/anonymouslanding.jsp" title="Link: http://grocery.walmart.com/usd-estore/m/home/anonymouslanding.jsp" target="_blank" data-cms-ai="3">online
grocery</a> for the next five years or more  if he can keep his sister at bay.
Im just worried about my job, he joked.<o:p></o:p></p></div>

<article id="art5" class="article-container" data-title="What is Seamless Shopping? Q&A with CEO Doug McMillon" data-permalink="http://blog.walmart.com/business/20160420/what-is-seamless-shopping-q-a-with-ceo-doug-mcmillon?reading-list=00000152-b329-d300-a153-bfbbe63b0000">
    <div class="article-header">
        <div class="article-header-inner">
            <a href="http://blog.walmart.com/topics/business" class="article-topic-title category" data-cms-ai="4">Business</a><h1 class="article-title">What is Seamless Shopping? Q&amp;A with CEO Doug McMillon</h1>

            <div class="article-byline">
                <div class="article-byline-text">
                    <p class="article-byline-author">By
                        <a href="http://corporate.walmart.com/search?q=Walmart Staff" data-cms-ai="4">Walmart Staff</a>
                    </p>

                    <p class="article-byline-text"></p>
                    <p class="article-byline-date date">April 20, 2016</p>
                </div>
            </div>
        </div>

        <div class="article-lead-media">    
    <img src="http://cdn.corporate.walmart.com/dims4/WMT/ed78276/2147483647/crop/5184x2249%2B0%2B719/resize/1300x564%3E/quality/90/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fcdn.corporate.walmart.com%2F27%2F46%2Fba532f7a45279f96764c038e4a0a%2Fwalmart-100715-218.jpg" class="article-lead-img" data-size="blogPost_1300x564"></div>
</div>

    <div class="article-body">
        <div class="article-content">
            <p class="MsoNormal"><i>The following conversation with Walmart CEO Doug
McMillon is excerpted from our 2016 Annual Report, which was released today.
<a class="" href="http://www.corporatereport.com/walmart/2016/ar/" target="_blank" title="Link: http://www.corporatereport.com/walmart/2016/ar/" data-cms-ai="4">Read the report</a>.</i></p><div class="videoContainer enhancement ">
	<iframe src="//www.youtube.com/embed/eTu0dMNcDHo?rel=0&wmode=opaque&showinfo=0&autohide=1" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe>
</div><p class="MsoNormal"><b>Q: Were
now living in a new period of retail disruption, largely driven by rapid
technological advances, and that change is likely to accelerate even further. What
does the future look like for Walmart?</b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><b>A:
</b>Our
work starts and ends with the customer. Technology has changed customer
expectations. Customers used to compare us with the store down the street; now
they compare us with the best online shopping experience. And beyond retail, they
compare us with every business they interact with in their lives. They compare
our pickup experience to the speed and friendliness of the best drive-through.
They compare our checkout process to the ease of paying with an app.</p><p class="MsoNormal">Customers should be able to shop on
their own terms  in a great store or club, with a quick pickup stop on the way
home from work, or with items reliably arriving at the front door. And
customers want to have some money left over to put toward their priorities: an
experience together as a family, a special gift every once in a while, or
savings for a rainy day.<br></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">Retail is not just about putting items
on a shelf anymore. Its about fighting for our customers, cutting out the
hassles and advocating for them on price, too. Were moving beyond just selling
products to being the brand customers rely on to make their lives simpler and
more meaningful as they save money.</p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><b>Q: Youve
mentioned seamless shopping before. What does that mean, and how will Walmart
deliver?</b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><b>A:</b> It starts with unparalleled assets
that only Walmart has  our 2.3 million people; more than 11,500 retail locations;
e-commerce websites and apps; and a dynamic, optimized supply chain. But it
also requires new capabilities and fresh thinking.</p>

<p class="MsoNormal">This includes new digital tools for
customers and frontline associates, as well as back-end software and platform
work that benefits the entire enterprise. The use of data, algorithms, advanced
forecasting capabilities  and more  is of extreme strategic significance.</p>

<p class="MsoNormal">We will put these pieces together in a
way no one else can. <o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">Ultimately, customers dont care about
what channel theyre shopping in, or about how we deliver them a product or
service. They simply know theyre shopping with Walmart.</p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><b>Q: What is
Walmart already doing to make progress against this seamless shopping strategy?</b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><b>A:
</b>To
help our associates succeed and better serve our customers, weve made big
changes  including investing approximately <a class="" href="http://corporate.walmart.com/opportunity" data-cms-ai="4">$2.7 billion over two years in
higher wages, education and training</a> to make Walmart U.S. a better place to work
and shop. Were already seeing positive results: our fourth quarter of fiscal
2016 marked six consecutive quarters of positive comps and five straight
quarters of positive traffic at Walmart U.S. Everything were doing in
omnichannel depends on customers having great interactions with us in our
stores.</p><aside class="text-center">
    <style type="text/css">#i591312c98588498e9e591eedf075aae1{display:inline-block;overflow:hidden;position:relative;width:603px;height:nullpx;}</style><span id="i591312c98588498e9e591eedf075aae1"><img src="http://cdn.corporate.walmart.com/dims4/WMT/09a006a/2147483647/crop/2000x1333%2B0%2B0/resize/603x603%3E/quality/90/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fcdn.corporate.walmart.com%2Fd5%2F12%2Fbe298d86436cb85a05ec3768cb63%2Fan-associate-loads-groceries-into-the-trunk-of-a-vehicle-for-grocery-pickup.jpg" width="603" height="402" alt="A male associate loads groceries into the trunk of a vehicle for grocery pickup" data-size="blog-post-image"></span></aside>
<p class="MsoNormal">We're also accelerating e-commerce and
technology advances globally. We expanded <a class="" href="http://blog.walmart.com/innovation/20160413/sharing-the-love-for-grocery-pickup" title="Link: http://blog.walmart.com/innovation/20160413/sharing-the-love-for-grocery-pickup" data-cms-ai="4">Online Grocery shopping to new
markets</a>, ramped up in-store and in-club pickup, fully acquired the Chinese online
retailer Yihaodian, and began to add new mobile services such as <a class="" href="http://news.walmart.com/news-archive/2015/12/10/walmart-introduces-walmart-pay" title="Link: http://news.walmart.com/news-archive/2015/12/10/walmart-introduces-walmart-pay" data-cms-ai="4">Walmart Pay</a>.
We developed a technology platform that we can scale across the business. We
improved our fulfillment capabilities with new centers that are helping us get
orders to customers doors faster and more efficiently.</p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><b>Q: Its
clear what these strategic investments mean for customers. How will they affect
associates and the communities where Walmart operates?</b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><b>A: </b>As we work to win with our customers, we will also create a great place to
work. We will create tremendous opportunities for people from all walks of
life, with all kinds of skill sets and education levels. Were striving to
create a true meritocracy. No matter where you start from or what your unique
and special characteristics are, you can fulfill your potential here. We
believe in opportunity and that hard work, dedication and talent should be
rewarded.</p><aside class="pullquote">
    <p>We will create tremendous opportunities for people from all walks of life, with all kinds of skill sets and education levels.</p>
    </aside>



<p class="MsoNormal">We will also use our size, mindset and
policies and help make the world a better place. We create opportunity
throughout our global supply chain  on farms and in factories, by buying more
from women-owned businesses, by hiring veterans and by strengthening the retail
industry workforce.</p>

<p class="MsoNormal">We work to be more sustainable, both
in our own operations and in our supply chain. We have three big goals:
creating zero waste, running on 100 percent renewable energy and selling products
that sustain people and the environment. And we give back to the communities we
serve  supporting American manufacturing, preparing for and responding to
natural disasters and fighting hunger.</p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><b>Q: What
does this growth plan mean for investors?</b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><b>A: </b>We will win with a differentiated, disruptive strategy<b> </b>and a foundation
of operational excellence. As we do, we believe shareholders will benefit by
receiving above-average returns.</p>

<p class="MsoNormal">Although this will be another year of foundational
investments, we believe we will soon be growing faster than the retail market.
We are a growth company; we just happen to be a large one.</p>

<p class="MsoNormal">The road ahead will not always be
easy, but by being customer focused, hungry, fast and accountable, we will win
and have a good time doing it.<o:p></o:p></p></div>
